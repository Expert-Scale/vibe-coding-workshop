# MSA Playbook

*Anonymized teaching sample. Adapt to your team's actual positions before use.*

## Scope
This playbook covers Master Service Agreements as either **vendor** (we're buying) or **customer** (we're selling). The position differs — see `knowledge/clause-matrix.csv` for the deal-size-banded standard positions.

## Indemnification

### As Vendor (we sell)
- Cap at fees paid in the prior 12 months
- Carve-outs: confidentiality breach, IP infringement (capped separately at 2x fees), gross negligence, willful misconduct
- IP indemnity: customer-chosen remedies (replace / refund / modify)

### As Customer (we buy)
- Cap at 1x-2x fees depending on deal size (see clause-matrix)
- Push for fees-plus-insurance for deals >$1M
- Reject "to the maximum extent permitted by law" without a numeric cap (red flag)

## Limitation of Liability
- Mutual cap, expressed as a multiple of fees in the trailing 12 months
- Standard: 12 months fees (small deals), 24 months fees (mid-market), bespoke (large)
- Carve-outs (uncapped): confidentiality breach, indemnification obligations, IP infringement, gross negligence, willful misconduct
- Never agree to unlimited consequential damages

## Data Protection
- Data breach notification: **72 hours** from confirmed incident (non-negotiable)
- Sub-processor list required, with right to object to new ones
- Data residency: US/EU only unless explicitly approved
- Right to audit: annual, 30-day notice minimum

## Payment Terms
- Net 30 standard
- Net 60 only with CFO approval
- Late fees: 1.5% per month or max legal rate, whichever lower

## Termination
- For convenience: 30-60 days notice depending on deal size
- For cause: 30 days cure period for material breach
- Survival: confidentiality, IP, indemnification, limitation of liability, accrued payment obligations

## Warranties
### As Vendor
- 90-day functional warranty on initial delivery
- Services performed in workmanlike manner per industry standards
- No "fit for particular purpose" without explicit acceptance criteria

### As Customer
- Don't accept "as is" without explicit risk pricing
- Reject "best efforts" without defined metrics

## When to Escalate
- Any uncapped liability
- Indemnification obligations with no carve-outs for receiving party fault
- Source code escrow required (red flag)
- Most-Favored-Nation pricing tied to other customers
- Term longer than 3 years with no termination-for-convenience right
- Governing law outside acceptable list
