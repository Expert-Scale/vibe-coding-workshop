# NDA Playbook

*Anonymized teaching sample. Adapt to your team's actual positions before use.*

## Term Length
Our standard NDA term is **2 years from the effective date**. We do not sign perpetual NDAs. For outbound NDAs to small parties (individuals, consultants), 1 year is acceptable.

## Definition of Confidential Information
Acceptable: information that is either
- Marked or identified as confidential at the time of disclosure, OR
- Would reasonably be understood as confidential given the context.

**Reject:** purely subjective definitions ("anything we deem confidential"). **Reject:** "any information disclosed" without a reasonableness qualifier.

## Required Carve-Outs
Standard exclusions from confidentiality:
- Information that is publicly known through no fault of the receiver
- Information independently developed without reference to the disclosing party's confidential information
- Information lawfully obtained from a third party
- Information required to be disclosed by law (with prompt notice to the discloser when permitted)

## Return / Destruction
At termination or upon request: return or destroy within **30 days**. One archival copy is permitted for compliance retention.

## Governing Law
- **Preferred:** Delaware
- **Acceptable:** California, New York
- **Escalate:** anything else

## Mutual vs. One-Way
Default to mutual (both parties protect each other's confidential information). A one-way NDA against us is a **red flag** — escalate unless there's a clear reason (e.g., we're the recipient of trade secrets for evaluation purposes).

## Term After Termination
Confidentiality obligations survive termination for **3 years** for general confidential information, or as long as the information remains a trade secret for trade-secret material.

## When to Escalate
- Counterparty demands a unilateral NDA in their favor
- Term longer than 5 years requested
- No reasonable carve-outs accepted
- Liquidated damages provisions
- Governing law outside the acceptable list
- Definition of confidential information is unreasonably broad after pushback
- Counterparty is in a jurisdiction with weak IP protection
