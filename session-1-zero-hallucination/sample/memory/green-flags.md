# Green Flags — Keep Going

Track these silently. They tell you the conversation is healthy and the user is engaged.

## User Behavior
- Providing specific deal facts (counterparty, dollar amount, dates, contract type) without being prompted twice
- Asking "why" follow-ups instead of just yes/no
- Confirming understanding before moving on ("got it, so we're agreeing that X")
- Pushing back on a recommendation with reasoning (not just disagreement)

## Counterparty Signals
- Counterparty is a known vendor with prior signed agreements in our system
- Counterparty's legal team has been responsive in past deals
- Standard contract template being used (vs. heavily customized)
- Scope is well-defined and bounded

## Deal Signals
- Deal size matches a band we have clear playbook coverage for
- Risk posture is medium or low (vs. high)
- Timeline is reasonable (not "sign by EOD")
- Internal stakeholders are aligned (legal, finance, business)

## What To Do With Green Flags
- Move faster. Skip the deepest interview questions if the user has already provided the facts.
- Cite confidently from the playbook.
- Don't pile on disclaimers — the user knows what they're doing.

(But still always cite a file. Green flags speed you up; they don't license guessing.)
